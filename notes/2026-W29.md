## CISO Series / Defense in Depth — Protecting AI Agents in O365 and Google Workspace (2026-07-16)
- The panel argued that handing AI agents broad data access without a real access-control protocol is "a recipe for disaster" — OWASP guidance gets invoked as a stand-in, but it was never designed as a security/authorization protocol, so teams are leaning on a framework that doesn't actually constrain what an agent can reach.
- A recurring theme was "architecture over rollout": speed-to-deploy AI agents in O365/Workspace is outpacing the access-model design work, so security teams inherit whatever permission sprawl existed before the agent was added ("pre-existing conditions" carry straight through).
- The panel distinguished access from legitimacy — an agent (or a human) having a valid credential/scope to reach a mailbox or drive file doesn't mean that reach is appropriate, an important framing for scoping non-human identity reviews.

## ISACA Podcast — Modern Approach to Identity Security (2026-07-17)
- Donnie Carpenter (ISACA) and Christopher Hills (BeyondTrust Chief Security Strategist) argued that identity programs still built around locking down root/admin accounts are outdated — attackers now chase any path to privilege, not just the traditional superuser account.
- Hills pointed to fragmented, siloed point-tooling as the core weakness: security gaps split across endpoints, cloud, SaaS, third-party access, agentic AI, and non-human identities (NHIs) let attackers pick off the weakest-covered layer rather than needing to beat a single hardened control.
- The episode framed agentic AI and NHIs as the newest and least-mature privilege pathway, meaning identity programs that haven't extended privileged-access reviews to service accounts and AI agents have a growing blind spot.

[Episode link](https://www.isaca.org/resources/news-and-trends/isaca-podcast-library/modern-approach-to-identity-security)

## Audit, CISO & VAPT angle
This week's throughline is non-human/agentic identity as an under-governed privilege pathway: both ISACA and Defense in Depth independently flagged that AI agents and service accounts are getting data/system access without the review rigor applied to human privileged accounts. For audit engagements, this argues for extending privileged-access and least-privilege testing scope to explicitly cover AI agents and NHIs rather than treating them as out of scope. For board/leadership conversations, the "access isn't legitimacy" framing is a useful line for explaining why a valid credential isn't the same as an appropriate one. For VAPT engagements, this is a prompt to add agent/NHI permission-scoping checks (O365, Google Workspace, cloud IAM) to test plans, since fragmented tooling across environments is the specific gap attackers are described as exploiting.
