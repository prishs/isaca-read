# Security & Audit Digest — 2026-07-07

## Top items

### 1. BeyondTrust patches critical pre-auth bypass flaws in Remote Support/PRA
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** PAM appliances anchor remote-support and vendor-access controls; a pre-auth bypass undermines the trust chain auditors rely on when testing PAM effectiveness.
CVE-2026-40138 and CVE-2026-40139 (CVSS 9.2 each) let an attacker bypass access controls on Remote Support and Privileged Remote Access appliances (25.3.2 and earlier) under certain auth configurations. Cloud instances were patched in April; self-hosted customers must apply the April rollup or upgrade to 25.3.3+. ([The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/beyondtrust-patches-critical-auth.html))

### 2. Cisco confirms active exploitation of Unified CM SSRF flaw, KEV deadline overdue
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Core telephony infrastructure; the KEV remediation deadline has already passed, a live patch-compliance gap for auditors to flag.
CVE-2026-20230 is an unauthenticated SSRF in Cisco Unified CM/Unified CM SME chainable to root compromise (requires WebDialer enabled). Cisco confirmed real-world exploitation; CISA's June 28 KEV deadline has passed. ([BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-sets-urgent-deadline-to-fix-cisco-flaw-exploited-in-attacks/))

### 3. SharePoint deserialization RCE added to CISA KEV
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Widely deployed on-prem platform, exploitable by any authenticated low-privilege Site Member — a high-likelihood, high-impact patch item.
CVE-2026-45659 (CVSS 8.8) affects SharePoint Server Subscription Edition, 2019 and Enterprise 2016. Microsoft patched it in May; CISA confirmed active exploitation and set a July 4 federal deadline — already missed. ([The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/sharepoint-rce-cve-2026-45659-added-to.html))

### 4. Threat actors probe Gitea Docker auth-bypass flaw
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Shows how an insecure container-image default becomes instant internet-wide exposure — a lesson for reviewing self-hosted DevOps configurations.
CVE-2026-20896 (CVSS 9.8) let any network client impersonate any Gitea user via a spoofed `X-WEBAUTH-USER` header, because Gitea's Docker images trusted all reverse-proxy IPs by default. Sysdig saw first exploitation 13 days after disclosure; ~6,200 instances remain exposed. ([The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/threat-actors-probe-gitea-docker-flaw.html))

### 5. "JadePuffer" ransomware group automates full attack chain with an AI agent
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Board-level signal — attack velocity is no longer bound by human bandwidth, relevant to detection/response SLAs and budgets.
Researchers documented what they call the first fully agentic ransomware operation: an LLM-driven agent handled recon, credential theft, lateral movement, privilege escalation and database extortion largely unattended, entering via known Langflow and Nacos CVEs. ([BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/jadepuffer-ransomware-used-ai-agent-to-automate-entire-attack/))

### 6. FBI seizes NetNut proxy platform tied to 2-million-device Popa botnet
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Removes shared criminal infrastructure used for credential stuffing, account takeover and scraping — relevant to fraud and threat-intel teams.
The FBI and IRS-CI, with Google, Lumen, Shadowserver and others, seized domains behind NetNut, a proxy service (Alarum Technologies) linked to the Popa botnet. Google saw 316 distinct threat-actor clusters abusing NetNut nodes in one week in June. ([Krebs on Security](https://krebsonsecurity.com/2026/07/fbi-seizes-netnut-proxy-platform-popa-botnet/))

### 7. France's ANSSI to require quantum-safe encryption for certification from 2027
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** First hard national deadline forcing post-quantum migration into vendor certification and procurement — a driver for crypto-inventory audits.
France's cyber agency will stop certifying products lacking quantum-resistant encryption from 2027, targeting full adoption by 2030, citing "harvest now, decrypt later" risk to government/CNI procurement. ([SC Media](https://www.scworld.com/brief/france-accelerates-transition-to-post-quantum-encryption))

### 8. Suspected China-nexus actors spoof India's tax authority to deploy DcRAT
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Continued abuse of tax-season lures and fileless RAT delivery — useful for phishing-simulation content and detection tuning.
A campaign tracked as "Operation DragonReturn" used spear-phishing impersonating India's Income Tax Department to deliver DcRAT, a cross-platform RAT, to taxpayers and businesses. ([The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/suspected-china-nexus-hackers-use-fake.html))
