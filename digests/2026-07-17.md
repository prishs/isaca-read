# Security & Audit Digest — 2026-07-17

## Top items

### 1. SonicWall SMA 1000 zero-days under active exploitation, KEV deadline today
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Remote-access appliances are prime perimeter targets, and today is the federal patch deadline — any SMA 1000 fleet still unpatched should be treated as an active incident.
SonicWall disclosed active exploitation of two zero-days in SMA 1000 series appliances: CVE-2026-15409 (CVSS 10.0, SSRF) and CVE-2026-15410 (code injection), both added to CISA's KEV catalog on 2026-07-14 with a 2026-07-17 remediation deadline. One flaw can enable arbitrary command execution. ([source](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/two-sonicwall-sma-1000-zero-days.html))

### 2. CISA adds SharePoint and FortiSandbox flaws to KEV
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Two more hard deadlines stack onto this week's SonicWall/Oracle/KNX cycle — confirm patch tracking covers SharePoint on-prem and Fortinet sandbox appliances specifically.
CISA added CVE-2026-58644 (SharePoint deserialization, due 2026-07-19) and CVE-2026-39808/CVE-2026-25089 (FortiSandbox OS command injection, due 2026-07-19) to KEV on 2026-07-16; a separate SharePoint flaw, CVE-2026-56164, was added 2026-07-14 with today's deadline. ([source](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2026/07/16/cisa-adds-three-known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog))

### 3. Microsoft ships record 622-flaw Patch Tuesday, two zero-days under active attack
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** The largest Patch Tuesday on record (plus 427 Chromium/Edge CVEs) is a compliance-tracking load spike — confirm the two actively exploited zero-days are prioritized ahead of the general rollout.
Microsoft's July release covered 622 of its own CVEs, more than triple June's prior high, including 62 rated critical and two flaws already under active exploitation. ([source](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/microsoft-patches-record-622-flaws.html))

### 4. n8n token-exchange flaw let attackers log in as users from another issuer
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** A textbook federated-identity misconfiguration — audit any multi-issuer OIDC/OAuth setup for `iss`-claim validation, not just `sub`.
On n8n Enterprise instances trusting multiple external token issuers, the platform matched incoming JWTs to local accounts by `sub` claim alone while ignoring `iss`, letting a valid token from one issuer authenticate as a user from another. ([source](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/n8n-token-exchange-flaw-could-let.html))

### 5. OAuth client ID spoofing used to validate stolen Microsoft Entra credentials
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Attackers are confirming stolen credentials while evading standard sign-in telemetry — add detection for anomalous OAuth client authentication rather than relying on MFA coverage alone.
At least two threat actors are using a novel OAuth client ID spoofing technique to enumerate accounts and validate stolen credentials in Microsoft Entra ID environments without triggering normal logging. ([source](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/oauth-client-id-spoofing-lets-attackers.html))

### 6. Two Scattered Spider members sentenced to 5.5 years for £29M TfL hack
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** A concrete post-incident/legal outcome from a major social-engineering breach — useful precedent for board reporting on ransomware risk and law-enforcement engagement.
Owen Flowers (18) and Thalha Jubair (20) were each sentenced on 2026-07-16 for the 2024 Transport for London hack, which knocked out 148 systems and forced all 27,000 TfL staff offline. ([source](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/two-scattered-spider-hackers-get-55.html))

### 7. Coca-Cola's Fairlife ransomware attack halts US dairy production
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** OT/production-line impact from a ransomware hit at a major consumer-goods subsidiary — a live example for business-continuity and OT-segmentation reviews.
Coca-Cola disclosed that a ransomware attack on its Fairlife dairy subsidiary disrupted operations and temporarily halted US production. ([source](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/coca-cola-says-fairlife-ransomware-attack-halts-us-dairy-production/))

### 8. Daxin rootkit resurfaces in Taiwan alongside new Stupig pre-login backdoor
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Dormant APT kernel-mode tooling reactivating after 4+ years in a manufacturing target is a signal for OT/critical-manufacturing threat hunts and firmware-integrity checks.
The Daxin kernel-mode rootkit (srt64.sys), previously linked to a China-based actor, resurfaced in a Taiwan manufacturing firm alongside a previously unreported pre-login SYSTEM backdoor dubbed Stupig. ([source](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/daxin-resurfaces-in-taiwan-alongside.html))

### 9. 23andMe to pay $18 million in new genetics data breach settlement
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Regulators continue holding data custodians financially liable for weak consent and data-protection controls — relevant precedent for any org handling biometric/genetic data.
23andMe agreed to an $18 million settlement with a coalition of 43 state attorneys general over its handling and protection of customer genetic data. ([source](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/23andme-to-pay-18-million-in-new-genetics-data-breach-settlement/))

### 10. BSI study: Enhanced Sign-in Security essential for Windows Hello, biometrics add no entropy
**Why it matters for audit/CISO/VAPT:** Direct, actionable configuration guidance from a national security agency — a concrete checklist item for endpoint-authentication policy reviews.
Germany's BSI published a deep-dive on Windows Hello for Business finding that Enhanced Sign-in Security (ESS) is essential for TPM-backed protection, and that biometric enrollment doesn't add cryptographic entropy versus a PIN. ([source](https://isc.sans.edu/podcastdetail/10012))
